Guiding Principles

What makes this podcast different?

1. Interdisciplinary Approach

Reality doesn’t respect the boundaries of our disciplines; neither will this podcast.

2. Focus on Virtue

Wisdom is not just about thinking soundly but also living well. Expect conversations that explore the practical alongside the theoretical.

3. Embracing Dissent

“He who knows only his own side of the case knows little of that.” — J.S. Mill

Expect conversations where opposing and alternative views are steel‑manned, not straw‑manned.

God and Religion

Exploring God, faith, meaning, and religious life. We probe not just whether God exists, but what follows if He does.

  • Reason, revelation, experience
    • What counts as evidence—reason, revelation, experience?
    • What kind of knowledge of God is possible?
  • Suffering, freedom, justice
    • Free‑will defense, soul‑making, and justice.
    • Moral vs. natural evil—do they need different answers?
  • Apophatic & cataphatic
    • Apophatic vs. cataphatic paths—reasoning and awareness.
    • Theosis and sanctification—where emphases meet and diverge.
  • From Galileo to gene editing
    • Conflict or synergy?
    • Where are the proper boundaries of each domain?
🧠

The Mind and AI

Where code meets cognition. We examine consciousness, agency, and the limits of computation—are we confusing fluent output for lived awareness?

  • Hard problem, qualia, measurement
    • Is consciousness fundamental or emergent?
    • How do we measure it—report vs. behavior?
  • Turing tests, Chinese Room, agency
    • Passing tests vs. genuine understanding
    • Tool vs. agent vs. person—where’s the line?
  • Embodiment, symbols, predictive processing
    • Are bodies required for minds?
    • Symbolic vs. neural—hybrids that work?
  • Phenomenal vs. access consciousness
    • Can awareness exist without report?
    • Metacognition and self‑models
❤️

Health and Wellbeing

Evidence‑based habits, longevity, fitness, and flourishing.

  • Mediterranean, low‑carb, plant‑based
    • Evidence quality vs. anecdotes—how to weigh them?
    • Personalization, adherence, and biomarkers
  • ApoB, LDL‑C, blood pressure
    • Zone 2 vs. HIIT—what’s optimal for heart health?
    • Lipids, inflammation, and lifestyle levers
  • Protein, progressive overload, sleep
    • Minimum effective dose across decades
    • Strength vs. hypertrophy—do you need both?
  • Over/underdiagnosis, stimulants, environment
    • Medical vs. social models—where’s the tension?
    • Focus strategies beyond medication

Practical Wisdom

Money, productivity, relationships, and parenting. We translate first principles into everyday decisions—because philosophy that won’t do the dishes is just theory.

  • Compounding, risk, diversification
    • Passive vs. active—what survives the data?
    • How do incentives shape markets?
  • Capture, review, deep work
    • Systems that survive busy seasons
    • Energy management vs. time management
  • Attachment, commitment, repair
    • Rituals that deepen bonds
    • Conflict styles and repair attempts
  • Boundaries, warmth, play
    • Authoritative vs. permissive—what outcomes?
    • Household rhythms that actually stick

FAQ

Why “Aletheia”?

Aletheia (uh‑LAY‑thee‑uh) is Greek for “truth.”

What’s the show format?

One 60–90 minute conversation per episode, plus occasional short “Field Notes” on a single idea or practice.

How do you handle disagreement?

We welcome it. We steel‑man each other’s views, ask for the best evidence, and keep it human. Clarity over victory.

How can I support the show?

Join the list, share an episode when it lands, or introduce us to a guest we should meet.
