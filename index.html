    About your Host, Newman Nahas

    Curious about all the human things.

    Newman's academic path looks like someone who couldn't make up his mind. As it turned out, that was a feature, not a bug. Newman took coursework in thirteen different subjects and earned a Rhodes Scholarship to Oxford, where he waded into the waters where philosophy, religion, and science intersect. He then went to Harvard Law School, where he studied law and served as a teaching fellow for Michael Sandel's iconic class, Justice.

    The constant throughout: a relentless curiosity that won't quit. Instead of fighting it any longer, Newman has embraced it, building a podcast and community for interdisciplinary discussions around it.

    Here, he sits down with brilliant minds from different worlds to explore what makes us human—the profound and the mundane, the eternal and the everyday. One conversation might dissect ancient texts; the next, cutting-edge neuroscience. From psychology to capital markets, from the philosophy of fitness to sacred spaces and laboratory benches. This is a podcast about all the human things.
