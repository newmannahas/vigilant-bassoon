About Newman Nahas

Curious about all the human things.

Newman Nahas is curious about all the human things. As an undergraduate, he took classes across 13 disciplines and was awarded a Rhodes Scholarship to study at the University of Oxford, where he focused on questions at the intersection of philosophy, religion, and science.

He later attended Harvard Law School, working on the philosophy of law and its relationship to prevailing ethical theories. Throughout, Newman has had a hard time sitting still in any one discipline. (He also has ADHD—so he’s had a hard time sitting still, period.)

This podcast is Newman's response to that persistent, wide‑ranging curiosity. Its aim is to take on the big human questions by interviewing thought leaders across different disciplines and perspectives. The conversations will take you from metaphysics to gym mats, from ancient texts to modern labs, and everywhere in between.

FAQ

Why “Aletheia”?

Aletheia is Greek for “truth” in the sense of what’s unconcealed or brought to light.

What’s the show format?

One 60–90 minute conversation per episode, plus occasional short “Field Notes” on a single idea or practice.

How do you handle disagreement?

We welcome it. We steel‑man each other’s views, ask for the best evidence, and keep it human. Clarity over victory.

How can I support the show?

Join the list, share an episode when it lands, or introduce us to a guest we should meet.
