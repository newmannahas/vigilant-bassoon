    About Newman Nahas

    Curious about all the human things.

    Newman Nahas has never met a subject he didn’t want to explore. As an undergraduate, he studied across 13 different fields and was awarded a Rhodes Scholarship to study at Oxford, where he waded into the waters where philosophy, religion, and science intersect. He then attended Harvard Law School, continuing the journey into the philosophy of law while serving as a teaching fellow for Michael Sandel’s celebrated course on Justice.

    Throughout, one constant remained: an almost restless curiosity (and, yes, the ADHD didn’t exactly hurt the “restless” part). Rather than fight it, Newman built a podcast around it. Expect conversations with leading thinkers from different worlds about the human things—big and small. These discussions leap from metaphysics to gym mats, from ancient texts to modern labs, from boardrooms to churches.
