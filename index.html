God and Religion

Exploring God, faith, meaning, and religious life.

  • Natural theology, revelation, experience
    • What counts as evidence—reason, revelation, experience?
    • What is God like—goodness, simplicity, personhood?
  • Suffering, freedom, justice
    • Moral vs natural evil—do they need different answers?
    • Free‑will defense vs soul‑making theodicy?
  • Atonement, apophatic/cataphatic, theosis
    • Filioque & councils—why did they matter?
    • Theosis vs sanctification—two pathways?
  • From Galileo to gene editing
    • Conflict or complementarity—what models fit best?
    • Where are the proper boundaries of each domain?

The Mind and AI

Where code meets cognition—unraveling the mysteries of mind and machine.

  • Hard problem, qualia, global workspace
    • Is consciousness fundamental or emergent?
    • How do we measure it—report vs behavior?
  • Turing tests, Chinese Room, agency
    • Passing tests vs genuine understanding
    • Tool vs agent vs person—where’s the line?
  • Embodiment, symbols, predictive processing
    • Are bodies required for minds?
    • Symbolic vs neural—hybrids that work?
  • Phenomenal vs access consciousness
    • Can awareness exist without report?
    • Metacognition and self‑models

About Newman Nahas

Interdisciplinary Voyager

I'm Newman Nahas. I studied philosophy and religion at Oxford as a Rhodes Scholar. But throughout my life, I have struggled to sit still within the boundaries of any discipline. Prior to grad school, I ended up taking classes in 13 disciplines.

Aletheia Conversations is my response to that struggle. It's a space for exploring the big philosophical questions of life and also the mundane and practical questions too.
